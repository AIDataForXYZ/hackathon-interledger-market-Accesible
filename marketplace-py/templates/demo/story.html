Preserving the world's languages,
one job at a time

An Interledger-powered marketplace that connects indigenous language speakers with organizations that need their skills — designed voice-first for communities with limited written language.

The Problem
Half of the world's 7,000 languages will disappear this century

Indigenous communities are cut off from digital work, healthcare information, and civic participation because content isn't available in their languages.

🇪🇹

68 indigenous languages in Mexico

Many with fewer than 1,000 speakers remaining. The speakers exist — but there's no economic infrastructure to pay them for their language skills.

🚨

Health information doesn't arrive

Diabetes prevention pamphlets, vaccination campaigns, patient rights — all in Spanish. Monolingually indigenous patients can't access basic health information.

✏️

Literacy barriers block digital work

Many indigenous language speakers have limited written literacy — not because they lack intelligence, but because their languages were historically oral. Traditional freelance platforms exclude them.

Our Solution
A marketplace built for how people actually communicate

Voice-first design. Fair payments through Interledger. Real work that preserves languages while paying speakers fairly.

🎧

Voice-First Interface

Every button, label, and instruction can be narrated in the user's language. Users can apply for jobs and submit work through audio recordings — no writing required. We already have Otomi (Hñähñu) narration throughout the UI.

💰

Interledger Payments

Escrowed payments via Open Payments protocol. Funders authorize payment when posting a job. Creators receive funds directly to their Interledger wallet when work is accepted. No intermediary takes a cut.

🌍

Real-World Jobs

Not charity — real paid work. Translate health pamphlets. Record election radio spots. Transcribe grandmother's recipes. Create art from cultural traditions. Every job completed is a language act preserved.

Who Uses This
Organizations and creators who are already here

The marketplace connects two sides: organizations that need native-language content, and speakers who can create it.

Funders

Cooperatives — Product labels and training materials

Creators

Community interpreters who work in clinics and courts
Bilingual teachers who know how to explain complex ideas simply
Radio broadcasters with professional voice and recording equipment
Artisan-translators who weave textiles and translate documents
Linguists who do phonetic transcription and language documentation

What makes this different from Fiverr or Upwork? Those platforms require written literacy, bank accounts, and English. Our marketplace works through voice, pays through Interledger wallets, and serves communities where Spanish is a second language — if it's spoken at all.
