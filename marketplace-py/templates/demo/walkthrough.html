What Grant Reviewers Should Notice
Key differentiators in the demo

Voice Throughout

Audio player icons appear next to navigation items, form labels, and buttons. Each can play a narration in the user's selected language. This isn't text-to-speech — these are real recordings by native speakers.

Real Translation Content

The completed jobs contain actual translations — Otomí, Quechua, Zapoteco, Chinanteco, Triqui, Nahuatl. These aren't lorem ipsum. Review a submission to see a translator's cultural notes explaining why they adapted certain phrases.

Multi-Stakeholder Design

5 different funder organizations (NGO, clinic, cooperative, government, research) show this isn't a single-use tool. Each has different needs but the same platform serves them all.

Full Job Lifecycle

Jobs exist at every stage: recruiting (accepting applications), selecting (choosing workers), submitting (work in progress), reviewing (deliverables ready), and complete (accepted and paid). The entire pipeline works.

Audio Submissions

Some jobs require audio deliverables — a radio spot, a clinic voicemail. The submitted work includes actual Otomí audio recordings that a funder can listen to before accepting.

Cultural Adaptation Notes

Translators don't just translate literally — they explain their choices. "I adapted 'sacred' because in Zapoteco we express that concept differently." This shows the human expertise that machines can't replicate.
